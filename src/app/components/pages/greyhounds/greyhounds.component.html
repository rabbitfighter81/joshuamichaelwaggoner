Greyhounds

Greyhound Rescue We love Greyhound Rescuers and wish for the termination of greyhound racing

My family and I have a lot of love for greyhounds, not only because they are such wonderful dogs, but also because unfortunately, many of them experience abuse and trauma if they are are coming off the racing track. After leaving life on the track they are in immediate need of a home, love, stability, and patience. Many of these poor dogs come with a range of problems like worms, malnutrition, drug use, PTSD, and severe anxiety - all ranging from mild to extreme on a case by case basis.

Rescuing these dogs, who are so loveable, sweet, and kind, from kill shelters, or death from neglect and abuse, is so important that we have taken it up as a family cause. We plan on adopting and fostering greyhounds for the rest of our lives because they teaches us more about love, patience, and trust that we could ever learn from another human. Despite the challenges and hardships they have experienced these dogs love unconditionally. They become your shadow, they make great bedtime snuggle buddies, and intuitively they will pick you up when you are down, be with you when you are happy, and lick up all the food you drop on the floor. Greyhounds also love to sleep and can be characterized as couch potatoes.

For now, we just have Raven, but once we get settled in Spokane, we want to grow our greyhound family.
